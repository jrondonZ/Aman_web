Features

Everything that affects your safety, on one map.

Six live layers, continuously refreshed and de-duplicated, so you see signal instead of a firehose of raw alerts.

Live crime map

Incident-level reports from Connecticut's NIBRS feed, plotted the moment departments publish them. Filter by type — larceny, assault, burglary, motor-vehicle theft — and see the last 24 hours or the last 12 months.

Offender registry

The public sex-offender registry, mapped to addresses near you and sourced from the federal NSOPW system. Set a radius around your home or your child's school and know exactly who is required to register nearby.

Amber & missing alerts

Active Amber alerts and missing-person bulletins for Connecticut, pulled from NamUs and the National Weather Service CAP feed. You get the vehicle, the description and the region — not just a buzz on your phone.

Amanix AI assistant

Ask in plain English — "How's crime trending on my street?" or "Is the playground on Park St safe right now?" Amanix reads every layer and answers with grounded, source-backed context. Meet Amanix →

Personalized alert radius

Draw a radius around home, work or school and choose what's worth a notification. Aman watches it for you and pings you only when something inside your circle changes — quiet by default, loud when it matters.

Community reporting

See something off? Drop a neighbor note — a broken streetlight, a suspicious vehicle, an unlit underpass. Reports are time-stamped and shown alongside official data so the whole block stays in the loop.

Data sources

Built on official, verified sources.

Aman doesn't scrape rumors. Every layer traces back to a government feed or a recognized provider — refreshed continuously, with the source shown on every record.

Statewide incident-based crime reporting from the Connecticut Open Data portal.

NSOPW registry

GDELT news

High-resolution basemaps and street data powering the map you actually look at.

FAQ

Questions, answered.

Is Aman free?
Yes. Aman is free for Connecticut residents — the live map, all six data layers, your alert radius and Amanix are included. No credit card, no trial clock.
Where does the data come from?
From official and verified sources: Connecticut's NIBRS crime data on the CT Open Data portal, the federal NSOPW sex-offender registry, NamUs and the NWS CAP feed for missing-person and Amber alerts, GDELT for local news, and Esri / OpenStreetMap for basemaps. Every record shows its source.
Is my location private?
Your saved places live on your device and are used to filter what you see. We don't sell location data, and you can clear your saved spots at any time. Amanix only uses the area you ask about to answer your question.
Does it cover my town?
All 169 Connecticut towns are covered — from large cities like Hartford, New Haven, Stamford and Bridgeport down to small shoreline and rural towns. Coverage depth depends on what each department reports to the state feeds.
How accurate is the registry data?
Registry records come straight from NSOPW, the official federal source, and are refreshed continuously. As with any public registry, addresses can lag real-world moves, so Aman timestamps every record and labels its source. It's a tool for awareness — not a substitute for official verification.
Can Aman replace calling the police?
No. Aman is an intelligence and awareness tool, not an emergency service. For anything urgent, call 911. For non-emergencies, Aman surfaces the CT State Police line, 860-685-8000, right in the app.
